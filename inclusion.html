Inclusive & Safe Team Culture

5041 CyBear Robotics Training Module

Module Goals

By the end, team members should be able to:

  • Explain what “Everyone has a place here” looks like in daily team behavior.
  • Describe how inclusion, safety, and learning connect in 5041.
  • Choose actions that help new, quiet, younger, unsure, or different team members participate.
  • Respond professionally to mistakes, conflict, exclusion, harassment, or bullying.
  • Practice communication habits that build trust across technical and non-technical roles.
  • Commit to specific behaviors that make 5041 safer and more welcoming.

Student Needs

What students need from the team

Safety

  • Respectful language
  • No bullying or harassment
  • Clear ways to ask for help

Belonging

  • People are invited in
  • Roles are explained
  • Questions are treated as normal

Purpose

  • Everyone has useful work
  • Strengths are noticed
  • Growth matters more than status

When people feel safe, they ask better questions, take useful risks, and stay involved longer.

Daily Actions

How to make space for different team members

New Members

Explain acronyms, tools, roles, and why the team does things a certain way.

Quiet Members

Invite ideas without forcing someone to perform in front of the whole group.

Younger Members

Give real jobs with support instead of only cleanup or watching.

Experienced Members

Ask them to teach, document, and make room instead of taking over.

Communication

Inclusive communication is specific and respectful

Helpful

  • “Want me to show you how this works?”
  • “What part do you want to try?”
  • “Let’s test that idea.”
  • “Can you explain your reasoning?”
  • “I made that mistake too.”

Harmful

  • “Just let me do it.”
  • “Everyone knows that.”
  • “That’s a dumb question.”
  • “You are not a build person.”
  • Jokes that target identity, ability, or background.
Mistakes

Safe teams make failure useful

Normalize

Everyone starts somewhere. Mistakes are expected in design, coding, wiring, awards, and outreach.

Repair

Fix the part, fix the process, and fix the relationship if someone was embarrassed or dismissed.

Document

Turn lessons learned into checklists, CAD notes, code comments, pit notes, and mentor reminders.

Roles and Access

Belonging means access to real work

A team can accidentally exclude people by:

  • Letting the same students use every tool.
  • Assuming quiet students are not interested.
  • Not explaining inside jokes or acronyms.
  • Making business, safety, media, or outreach seem less important than robot work.
  • Only asking friends to help with high-visibility tasks.

A team creates access by:

  • Rotating practice opportunities.
  • Pairing new and experienced members.
  • Explaining the “why” behind decisions.
  • Respecting all team roles as team success.
  • Keeping documentation useful for the next person.
Conflict

Professional conflict keeps people safe

Pause

Stop escalating. Keep your voice controlled and focus on the issue, not the person.

Clarify

Ask what happened, what is needed, and what decision has to be made next.

Escalate

If someone feels unsafe, threatened, excluded, or targeted, bring in a mentor immediately.

5041 expectation: Students should maintain a professional attitude and find diplomatic solutions.
